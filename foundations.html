05 · hō

The laws

The short list that settles arguments. Full text in the machine context.

Elevation: chrome sits below the content; the working surface is the brightest plane.
Never hue alone: every status indicator carries a glyph or shape; colour only reinforces.
Two temperaments: Okibi is cool ash, Akari is warm paper — never harmonize them.
Control text centers by construction: line-height 1 + symmetric padding in a flex-centered box.
Contrast: n9+ for readable text; n8/n7 are furniture. Measured pairings in the matrix.
Hue no-go zones: teal 180–230 and magenta 325–345 stay empty (exceptions: async 205, spell 200). Also never: italics (except comments), vanilla purple, pumpkin orange, muddy low-contrast colours, brown-tinted dark grounds, bright or void borders, decorative textures, emoji.
The aesthetic brief: modern Japanese — action anime, not sakura/pastel. Colours are a glow in the dark: sparse, meaningful, and legible as colour — restrained ≠ washed out. Loved anchors: viridian green, periwinkle, the ember, yamabuki gold as this/self, scarlet-derived reds, bold-for-globals.
