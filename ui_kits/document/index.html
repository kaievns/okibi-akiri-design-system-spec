01 · kata

The section pattern

かた · the form

Every section opens the same way: a title-card — kanji with the ember bar, a mono number-kicker, a mincho title, the reading — then a lede at 17.5px that says the one thing the section is for.

Body prose runs at 15.5/1.64 on the secondary tone, with bold reserved for the load-bearing phrase and code spans for identifiers. Keep paragraphs at measure (820px); let whitespace, not boxes, do the separating.

型

The callout closes the section. One kanji as the mark, ember-soft well, and the single claim the reader must leave with — never more than one callout per section.

03 · imashime

The do / don't pair

いましめ · discipline

Policy sections end in a paired rule card — ok-green pluses against ake-red dashes, both carried by the glyph as much as the colour.

◇ a document must

  • Open with the kicker, close the header with the signature rule.
  • Keep prose at measure; use middots for metadata: 01 · kata.
  • Set names as kanji + romaji + meaning on first use.

◇ it must not

  • Spend the ember on links, bullets, or decoration.
  • Use more than one callout per section, or two grounds per page.
  • Animate anything. Transitions are cuts.
